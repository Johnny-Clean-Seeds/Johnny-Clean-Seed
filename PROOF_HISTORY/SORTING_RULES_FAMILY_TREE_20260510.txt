SORTING RULES FAMILY TREE

Status:
Local durable save.
Not installed into ACTIVE_GUIDES yet.
Created during Gold Pan / Recycle Bin / recovered-file cleanup work.

Purpose:
Create a clean hierarchy for messy recovery work so the assistant does not dig item-by-item through raw clutter, lose gold, confuse judgment with fixing, or turn cleanup into deletion too early.

Core gold rule:
Pile-making is not judging.
Judging is not fixing.
Fixing is not deleting.

GRANDPARENT RULE:
Sorting Rules

Meaning:
Before judging, fixing, installing, archiving, or deleting messy material, sort it into visible piles by family, role, source, path, type, risk, status, and evidence quality.

Sorting is a control layer.
Sorting does not decide value.
Sorting does not change active files.
Sorting does not delete anything.

PARENT RULE:
Pile-First Recovery

Meaning:
When working through Recycle Bin material, lost folders, duplicate guides, proof-history clutter, recovered quarantine files, or messy evidence piles, do not inspect/fix/delete item-by-item in the raw mess.

First freeze the scene.
Then inventory.
Then make piles of like things.
Then review one pile at a time.
Then route each pile to the correct place.

CHILD RULES:

1. Scene Freeze
Stop movement first. Do not restore, delete, merge, rename, overwrite, or clean before the scene is captured.

2. Metadata Inventory
List items by name, original path, size, type, deleted date/time if available, source, and visible role. This is not content judgment.

3. Pile-Making
Group like with like:
README family
WRAP family
ALIGNMENT family
receipts
proof/report/manifest files
folder/container evidence
script-like blocked evidence
unknown ore
duplicates/no-new-value
delete-ready clutter

4. Chain of Custody
Every recovered item needs a durable identity record: item number, original name, source path, recovery path, timestamp, type, hash when useful, and action taken.

5. Collision Safety
Same-name files must not be restored or copied into one flat folder. Use numbered folders or original-path-preserving folders.

6. One-Pile Review
Review one pile at a time. Do not jump between README, Wrap, Alignment, receipts, scripts, and folders unless the current pile is closed, parked, or yielded.

7. Value Judgment
After a pile is visible, classify items as:
active truth candidate
proof-history reference
gold bar candidate
nugget
dirty milk risk
duplicate/no-new-value
blocked evidence
unknown ore
delete candidate

8. Routing
Route judged material to:
ACTIVE_GUIDES review
PROOF_HISTORY
archive
parked reference
blocked evidence
duplicate/no-new-value
delete gate
human decision

9. Fixing Gate
Fixing means changing or improving the correct active file, rule, guide, wording, or workflow.
A judgment does not automatically authorize fixing.
Fixing requires scope, target, reason, approval if needed, and verification.

10. Delete Gate
Deleting is separate from sorting, judging, and fixing.
Deletion requires proof that the target is redundant, represented elsewhere, outside protected active source, and approved by exact path.

11. Closure Verify
After cleanup or deletion, verify the protected source, quarantine/proof files, active guides, and residue state. Close only after proof.

GRANDCHILD CHECKS:

For Scene Freeze:
- Is anything about to move?
- Is anything about to be deleted?
- Is anything about to overwrite same-name files?
- Has inventory happened yet?

For Pile-Making:
- Are items grouped by family?
- Are scripts blocked?
- Are folders treated as containers?
- Are duplicates separated from unique material?

For Value Judgment:
- Is this judgment based on metadata only or content review?
- Is the item current truth or just recovered evidence?
- Is this clean milk, dirty milk, duplicate, or unknown?

For Fixing Gate:
- What active file would change?
- Why would it change?
- What exact wording changes?
- What stays protected?
- What proves the fix worked?

For Delete Gate:
- What exact path is deleted?
- Is it outside ACTIVE_GUIDES, CURRENT_TRUTH_INDEX, PROOF_HISTORY, and quarantine?
- Is it already represented elsewhere?
- Is there a receipt?
- Is there a post-delete verify?

Control rule:
Do not let pile-making become judgment.
Do not let judgment become fixing.
Do not let fixing become deletion.
