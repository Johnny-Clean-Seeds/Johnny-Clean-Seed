PURPOSE:
Place helpful things where they actually help.
Use room maps, hanging boards, to-do lists, design plans, movable rails, and expansion rules so the workshop stays clean, free, and ready to grow without becoming bloat.

CORE RULE:
A helpful thing must be placed where it helps the job.
A map belongs in the room that needs orientation.
A checklist belongs where the miss happens.
A to-do board belongs where decisions get parked.
A warning belongs before the danger, not after it.
A tool belongs in the box for the job, not scattered across the floor.

DESIGN PLANS:

PLAN: SLIDING WALLS
Meaning:
Rooms can be resized or moved when a real new need appears.
Rule:
Move structure only after affected-area review and backup rope.

PLAN: ROLLING CARTS
Meaning:
Temporary tool kits for short jobs.
Rule:
Return cart after job. Do not let carts become permanent clutter.

PLAN: PEGBOARD HOOKS
Meaning:
Each tool has a home.
Rule:
If a tool has no home, create a home, merge it, or red-tag it.

PLAN: HANGING MAPS
Meaning:
Maps sit where the user/assistant needs them.
Rule:
A map that is not used becomes clutter and should move to reference.

PLAN: CLIPBOARD STACK
Meaning:
Job cards are temporary.
Rule:
Open clipboard cards must close, park, or become receipt.

PLAN: BACKUP ROPE
Meaning:
Before big change, tie a rope back to known-good state.
Rule:
No major structure change without backup, save, protection, and restore route.

PLAN: TEST FLOOR
Meaning:
Try models safely before install.
Rule:
Experiment evidence must state what happened exactly, even if it worked.

PLAN: EXPANDABLE HOUSE
Meaning:
The house may grow, but only by rooms, maps, and boxes that reduce friction or risk.
Rule:
Expansion must improve flow, not decorate confusion.

PLACEMENT RULES:
1. Put warnings before danger.
2. Put maps where routing happens.
3. Put to-do lists where waiting happens.
4. Put toolboxes near the job they serve.
5. Put evidence ledgers near pass/fail decisions.
6. Put red tags near clutter sources.
7. Put backup rope near expansion and write work.
8. Put public boundary checks near public output.
9. Put mule judgment board at the mule dock.
10. Put cleanup checklist in cleanup closet, not at the front door.

EXPERIMENT LOG RULE:
Every experiment must capture:
- what we tried
- why we tried it
- what was protected first
- what changed
- what happened
- what failed
- what worked
- what surprised us
- what to do next
- what to save, park, merge, or shed

GOOD RESULT NOTE:
A good result still needs notes.
A future bad note may need the old good-note details to repair, compare, or improve.
Do not lose the recipe for what worked.

FAIL RESULT NOTE:
A fail result still helps.
Capture exactly how it failed, because it may reveal the missing tool, wrong room, bad trigger, or better design.

BIG MOVE GATE:
Before any real structural change:
1. Name the move.
2. Name affected rooms/files/tools.
3. Back up/save/protect.
4. Name the rope back.
5. Run smallest safe experiment.
6. Capture exact result.
7. Decide keep, merge, park, shed, or retry.
8. Do not promote without proof.
