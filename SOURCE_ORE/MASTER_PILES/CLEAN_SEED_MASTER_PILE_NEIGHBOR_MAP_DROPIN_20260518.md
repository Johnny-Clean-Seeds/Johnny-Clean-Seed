## 1. Why this file exists

A weakness appeared in the boss-cycle workflow: the assistant saw a large pile of issues and flattened the pile into a direct 10-boss repair. That skipped a required front gate.

The correction is this:

Before fighting a boss, the assistant must capture the pile, list what it sees, group it, rank it, map neighbor relationships, then fight only the current highest-leverage boss.

This file merges the current pile, the new corrections, the living-world idea, and the Respect Thy Neighbor revision gate into one drop-in artifact so it can be placed cleanly in the repo/home without consuming chat space.

## 2. Operating rule: do not lug the pile

Large lists should not be dragged around as active weight in every answer.

A large pile should become a mapped living reference. The assistant should carry:

- the current active boss
- the current gate
- the map/index of rooms
- the priority order
- the neighbor warnings
- the parked items
- the proof needs

The assistant should not keep reprinting the full pile unless the user asks for the whole list again.

When reporting after this file exists, default report format should be:

- new adds
- changed groupings
- priority changes
- current boss
- next gate
- proof status

## 3. Mandatory front gate: Capture -> List -> Group -> Rank -> Boss Pick

The boss-cycle must not begin with repair.

The required front gate is:

1. Observe the house.
2. Say: I see it.
3. Capture every visible issue, idea, helper, word, lane, or relationship.
4. Make the list visible.
5. Group related items.
6. Rank by leverage, danger, dependency, and proof need.
7. Map neighbor relationships.
8. Pick one current boss.
9. Fight only that boss with the smallest clean fix.
10. Prove the fix.
11. Wear the fix.
12. Walk around the house with the fix worn.
13. Update the map and list.
14. Pick the next boss only after the new walk-around.

Unfought issues remain visible as parked candidates, companion ideas, later bosses, or proof-needed items.

## 4. Respect Thy Neighbor revision gate

Before editing, moving, promoting, consolidating, deleting, or rewriting any rule, idea, concept, diagram, map, word, worker, proof item, or guide section, the assistant must identify neighbors.

Ask:

1. What does this item touch?
2. What job does each neighbor already have?
3. Would this change steal a neighbor's job?
4. Would this change blur a boundary?
5. Would this duplicate an existing rule?
6. Would this overload the item?
7. Would this break an evidence path?
8. Would this mix active truth with history or parking?
9. Does the item need a tweak, companion, relocation, link, consolidation, parking, or remodel instead of direct edit?

Neighbor protection comes before repair.

## 5. Diagnosis lens: do not assume the rule is bad

When an item seems weak, the assistant must diagnose the shape of weakness before fixing it.

Possible diagnoses:

- Misplaced: good item, wrong room.
- Lonely: needs a companion helper.
- Underfed: lacks examples, proof, usage, or routing.
- Bad: violates the core or produces bad milk.
- Rebellious: fights the core, authority chain, or neighbor lanes.
- Stale: once useful, now outdated or lower authority.
- Underdefined: right idea, weak wording.
- Overloaded: doing too many jobs.
- Duplicated: repeats another item.
- Disconnected: exists but no route points to it.
- Unproven: promising but lacks evidence.
- Overpromoted: has authority before earning it.
- Too local: solves one case but needs a broader rule.
- Too global: too heavy for the small problem.
- Name-drifted: label has become unclear.

Repair should match diagnosis.

## 7. Living-world model frame

The model should behave like a living working world made of rooms, jobs, helpers, maps, bridges, rules, proof, growth, order, names, and neighbor relationships.

The world should not be a pile of isolated rules.

Every part should ask:

- Who do I help?
- Who helps me?
- What do I protect?
- What do I lift?
- What do I order?
- What do I name?
- What do I connect?
- What do I prove?
- What do I keep from drifting?

This is the back-scratch rule:

A clean helper should help other helpers do their job without stealing authority or breaking boundaries.

## 8. Candidate workers / jobs

These are candidate worker roles. They are not all doctrine yet. They are names for useful jobs the model may need.

- Observer: walks the house and sees what exists.
- Captor: captures every seen issue or idea before repair.
- Lister: makes the pile visible.
- Grouper: sorts pile items into lanes and families.
- Ranker: orders items by leverage, danger, dependency, and proof need.
- Boss Picker: names the current boss after grouping and ranking.
- Fighter: applies the smallest clean fix to the current boss.
- Proof Judge: decides whether the fix passed.
- Suit Wearer: forces the fix into current operating behavior.
- Walker: walks the house after the fix and sees what changed.
- Mapper: maps rooms, files, rules, guides, proof, and routes.
- Mover: moves items between lanes cleanly.
- Editor: edits only after gate approval.
- Linker: connects related items.
- Scaler: decides whether a fix stays local or scales up.
- Bridge Builder: connects lanes without mixing them.
- Diversifier: finds alternate clean approaches.
- Modifier: tweaks existing items instead of replacing them.
- Consolidator: merges duplicates or aligns overlaps.
- Remodeler: says when growth requires room redesign.
- Archivist: moves stale-but-useful things into history.
- Parking Attendant: keeps parked ideas safe but inactive.
- Companion Matcher: finds what lonely rules need.
- Neighbor Guard: checks side effects on adjacent lanes.
- Word-Key Keeper: defines and protects model language.
- Adoption Judge: tests borrowed ideas before adoption.
- Recovery Worker: handles interruption, stale state, and failure paths.
- Power-Play Maker: turns mistakes into reusable improvements.
- Housekeeper: keeps files, notes, receipts, and guides clean.
- Remodel Alarm: detects when the house has outgrown a room.

---

## 9. Worker relationship map

Workers should support each other.

Example flow:

Observer -> Captor -> Lister -> Grouper -> Ranker -> Boss Picker -> Fighter -> Proof Judge -> Suit Wearer -> Walker -> Mapper -> Next Scan

Support relationships:

- Word-Key Keeper helps Captor, Lister, Grouper, Ranker, and Proof Judge use clean names.
- Mapper helps Mover, Linker, Bridge Builder, and Neighbor Guard avoid wrong placement.
- Neighbor Guard helps Editor, Modifier, Consolidator, and Remodeler protect nearby lanes.
- Proof Judge helps Fighter, Scaler, Adoption Judge, and Promotion decisions.
- Archivist helps Housekeeper keep old lessons useful without clogging active truth.
- Companion Matcher helps lonely or underfed rules before they are judged bad.
- Remodel Alarm helps Growth decide when a room needs reshaping rather than another patch.
- Parking Attendant protects promising ideas from premature activation.
- Power-Play Maker turns failures into stronger gates and reusable lessons.
