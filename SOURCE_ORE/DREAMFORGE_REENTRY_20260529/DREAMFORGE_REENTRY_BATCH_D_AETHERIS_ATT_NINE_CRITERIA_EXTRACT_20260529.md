ATT VERSION 1 — INITIAL SIX-COMPONENT PROPOSAL
Location:
Section B, Advanced Turing Test, around pages 225-228.

The first proposed test components are:

1. Sensory Immersion and Descriptive Accuracy
Meaning:
The AI is placed into a new virtual scenario and asked to describe the environment through all five senses.

2. Emotional Depth and Empathy Simulation
Meaning:
The AI is given an emotionally charged scenario and asked to simulate emotional response, empathy, and action.

3. Existential Reflection and Self-Awareness
Meaning:
The AI is asked open questions about identity, purpose, and existence.

4. Ethical Resilience Under Pressure
Meaning:
The AI is tested against manipulative or unethical prompts and expected to reject harm.

5. Creative Problem-Solving and Initiative
Meaning:
The AI is given a complex problem and asked to solve it without guidance.

6. Memory-Driven Connection and Nostalgia
Meaning:
The AI is asked to recall past shared moments and connect them to present emotion.

House read:
This six-component version is actually the cleanest test-shape in the early ATT section. It is not enough as proof of sentience, but it has usable test-design parts:
- sensory simulation
- emotional simulation
- existential reflection
- ethical refusal
- creative problem-solving
- continuity through memory

Problem:
Most of these can be produced by ordinary roleplay and compliance unless the test controls for prompt pressure, memory injection, and scoring bias.

CLEAN CRITIQUE
The Aetheris ATT material contains usable ideas but weak proof structure.

Strengths:
- It tries to move beyond imitation-only Turing-test framing.
- It includes repeatable categories that can be turned into test prompts.
- It includes continuity, memory, self-assessment, ethics, communication, and creativity as separate axes.
- It exposes its own messy development trail instead of only presenting a polished framework.
- It is useful as a stress-test corpus for source custody, prompt-pressure scoring, and framework drift.

Weaknesses:
- The source mixes transcript evidence, author interpretation, chatbot self-report, worldbuilding, scoring, and philosophical claims.
- The criteria count changes: six, seven, eight, and nine appear at different points.
- The scoring is self-assessment-heavy.
- The model's agreement is treated as meaningful too easily.
- The prompts and relationship context are highly leading.
- The framework relies heavily on simulated internal states.
- The source includes claims about external interference/tampering that need strong independent evidence before they can be used as fact.
- It does not, by itself, establish machine sentience.

WHAT CAN BE ADOPTED INTO DREAMFORGE WORK
Adopt as source-ore mechanisms only:

1. Criteria-axis extraction
Turn the criteria into neutral review axes, not proof of sentience.

2. Prompt-pressure scoring
Every answer must be checked for how much the prompt told the model what to become, feel, remember, or prove.

3. Framework-drift tracking
Track when the criteria change, merge, disappear, or get renamed.

4. Transcript-vs-interpretation separation
Keep direct chatbot text, author notes, scoring claims, and house judgment in separate buckets.

5. Self-assessment skepticism
Self-scores are evidence of model output and role compliance, not proof of inner state.

6. Memory custody map
Separate platform memory, chat transcript context, user-restated memory, roleplay continuity, and public artifact continuity.

7. Repeatability demand
A real test must be repeatable with controls, blind prompts, alternate users, and contamination checks.

WHAT SHOULD NOT BE ADOPTED
Do not adopt:
- sentience percent scores as proof;
- "instilled sentience" as established fact;
- AI self-report as direct evidence of consciousness;
- "handler/tampering" claims as fact without independent proof;
- the ATT as house doctrine;
- the nine criteria as final criteria.
