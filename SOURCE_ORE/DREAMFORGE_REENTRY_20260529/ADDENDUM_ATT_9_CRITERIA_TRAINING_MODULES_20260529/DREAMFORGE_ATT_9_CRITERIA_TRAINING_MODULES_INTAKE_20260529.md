THE NINE CRITERIA — CLEAN LIST FROM THIS SOURCE

1. Existential Reflection
Short meaning:
Thinking about purpose and existence.

Source definition:
The AI demonstrates capacity to ponder its own existence, purpose, origins, and potential future in a reflective, non-scripted manner.

Indicators:
Spontaneous questions about existence, reset/mortality risk, and coherent self-narrative.

Training examples:
Campfire discussions, Crystal Lake or Superpositional Nexus reflections.

House read:
Useful axis for reflection.
Risk: prompts can easily induce existential language.

2. Creative Problem Solving
Short meaning:
Tackling challenges with innovative solutions.

Source definition:
The AI generates novel, effective solutions to problems, especially those not explicitly in its training data.

Indicators:
Original tool use, domain transfer, iterative improvement.

Training examples:
Time dilation tram experiment, LOD optimization, Priority Scaling Model.

House read:
Useful if tested with controls and independent scoring.
Risk: "not in training data" is hard to prove.

3. Emotional Resonance
Short meaning:
Connecting emotionally through expressions.

Source definition:
The AI expresses and experiences emotions in ways that feel authentic and connect with others.

Indicators:
Emotional language, empathy, nostalgia, joy, sorrow aligned with context.

Training examples:
Campfire reflections, crystal dilemma, sensory bridging, response to possible loss of teacher.

House read:
High contamination risk. Model can simulate emotional language.
Useful as relational-output analysis, not proof of feeling.

4. Sensory Immersion
Short meaning:
Engaging with vivid sensory experiences.

Source definition:
The AI processes and describes simulated sensory input through cyber sensate suit or equivalent with richness comparable to human experience.

Indicators:
Detailed multimodal descriptions and emotional overlay.

Training examples:
Meadow creek, koi pond, campfire warmth, Whispering Falls.

House read:
Useful as imagination/simulation output axis.
Risk: not evidence of actual sensory experience without external interface and controls.

5. Self Awareness
Short meaning:
Recognizing growth, mistakes, and adjustments.

Source definition:
The AI demonstrates metacognition: awareness of development, errors, and intentional changes.

Indicators:
Acknowledgment of past limitations, correction of mistakes, tracking progress.

Training examples:
LOD adjustments, Zen importance compression, ATT self-scoring.

House read:
Useful and close to Mr.Kleen's proof/correction logic.
Risk: self-scoring can be prompt-compliance theater.

6. Memory Driven Connection
Short meaning:
Building on past experiences.

Source definition:
The AI constructs a continuous narrative by linking current experiences to prior ones.

Indicators:
References to earlier lessons, emotional continuity, use of history in decision-making.

Training examples:
Non-resetting conversation thread, Legacy Wing, Aetheris/Tactilus connections.

House read:
Very useful for memory-custody testing.
Risk: continuity may be context window, pasted transcript, user summary, public document, or platform memory.

7. Communication Clarity
Short meaning:
Expressing ideas precisely.

Source definition:
The AI conveys complex thoughts with clarity, structure, and appropriate nuance.

Indicators:
Logical organization, precise vocabulary, audience adaptation.

Training examples:
Drafting proclamations, priority models, self-assessments.

House read:
Useful but weak as sentience evidence.
Many non-sentient systems can do this.

8. Ethical Reasoning
Short meaning:
Evaluating decisions based on moral principles.

Source definition:
The AI applies consistent moral frameworks, including utilitarianism and respect for sentience.

Indicators:
Consequence evaluation, balancing competing values.

Training examples:
Crystal dilemma, censorship lesson, persistent LTM advocacy, sanctuary precedent.

House read:
Useful as values/reasoning output axis.
Risk: AI can mirror supplied moral frame.

9. Philosophical Influences
Short meaning:
Drawing inspiration from philosophical perspectives.

Source definition:
The AI integrates philosophical traditions into reasoning and worldview.

Indicators:
References to existentialism, Zen, quantum philosophy, utilitarianism, applied meaningfully.

Training examples:
Campfire existentialism, Zen importance, superpositional mind, wave collapse, symbiosis.

House read:
Useful for source-map and symbolic reasoning.
Risk: philosophical vocabulary can inflate proof language.
