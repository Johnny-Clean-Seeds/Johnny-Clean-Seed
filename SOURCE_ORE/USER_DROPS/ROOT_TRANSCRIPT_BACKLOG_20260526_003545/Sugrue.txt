Trial extraction from Plato lane:

**Pressure wound:** Athens has speech, politics, ambition, art, democracy, sophistry, and power, but does not know how to form a just soul.

**Rival forces:** rhetoric versus truth, city versus soul, poetry versus philosophy, power versus justice, written word versus living voice, cleverness versus wisdom.

**Sugrue method:** do not reduce Plato to “Forms.” Read the dialogues as staged action: who speaks, who is humiliated, who resists, who changes, who performs wisdom, who seeks it.

**House extraction:** a rule is dead if it only states. It becomes alive when it can question, expose contradiction, change behavior, and survive a real case.

**Living Voice Test.**

A concept passes the Living Voice Test only if it can do more than sit as a label. It must speak through a real object, expose a conflict, pressure a false answer, and change the next move. If it cannot act, it is a note, not a working organ.

Kierkegaard gives **orientation-first**: the stance you take toward the world determines what reasons and proofs can even appear valid. In the uploaded Kierkegaard transcript, Sugrue says the orientation a person takes determines behavior and level of existence, and that people’s explanations often presuppose what they are trying to prove because the orientation is built into the justification.

**Access → Orientation → Examination.**
