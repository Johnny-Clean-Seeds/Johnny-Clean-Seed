Chunk 7 — **Milankovitch cycles: Earth’s deep climate clocks**

Rope held. Old room dropped. We are still on measurable cycles first.

Now we move from sky-view cycles to **Earth-orbit climate cycles**. These are often called **Milankovitch cycles**. They are not daily, monthly, or yearly clocks. They are long background changes in Earth’s orbit and axial geometry that alter how sunlight is distributed across the planet over thousands of years.

NASA’s clean definition: Milankovitch cycles are changes in Earth’s orbit and orientation that affect the amount and distribution of solar radiation reaching Earth. The three main components are **eccentricity**, **obliquity**, and **precession**. ([NASA Science][1])

The first clock is **eccentricity**.

This means the shape of Earth’s orbit changes from more circular to more elliptical and back. Earth’s orbit is always nearly circular compared with many diagrams, but the small change matters over long climate windows because it changes the difference between Earth’s closest and farthest distances from the Sun. NASA describes eccentricity as Earth’s orbit changing shape, with major long cycles around the 100,000-year range and longer modulation as well. ([NASA Science][1])

Clean meaning:

**Eccentricity changes the shape of the orbit.**

It is not the cause of seasons. Seasons come mainly from axial tilt. The National Weather Service states directly that seasons are caused by Earth’s roughly 23.5-degree axial tilt, not by Earth being closer or farther from the Sun. ([National Weather Service][2])

The second clock is **obliquity**.

This is Earth’s axial tilt changing over time. Today Earth is tilted about 23.5 degrees, but the tilt varies slowly. NASA gives the obliquity cycle at about **41,000 years**. When tilt is greater, seasonal contrast tends to be stronger: summers receive more sunlight in the tilted hemisphere and winters less. ([NASA Science][1])

Clean meaning:

**Obliquity changes the strength of the seasonal tilt effect.**

It is not a new season. It is the deep modulation of season intensity over long time.

The third clock is **precession**.

We already touched precession as the slow wobble of Earth’s axis and drift of equinoxes against the stars. In the Milankovitch climate context, precession changes where seasons fall relative to Earth’s closest/farthest points from the Sun. NASA describes the combined precession cycle as averaging around **23,000 years** in climate forcing context. ([NASA Science][1])

Clean meaning:

**Precession changes the timing relationship between seasons and Earth’s orbital position.**

So the Milankovitch stack is:

**eccentricity** — orbit shape clock.
**obliquity** — axial tilt strength clock.
**precession** — seasonal timing / wobble clock.

Those three do not act separately in life. They braid. Their combined effect changes the distribution of sunlight by latitude and season, especially important for long glacial/interglacial climate pacing. NASA is careful here: Milankovitch cycles help explain long-term natural climate variations, but they do **not** explain current rapid warming; current warming is dominated by human-caused greenhouse gas increases, not orbital-cycle timing. ([NASA Science][1])
