## Difference That Matters

Boundaries Gate:
defines whose place is what.

Permission Gate:
decides whether an actor/object/path may enter or be trusted now.

Guest Boundary Gate:
applies boundary and permission rules to helper/mule/assistant/drop behavior.

Return-Home Gate:
checks cleanup and final placement after work.

Enforcement Ladder Gate:
handles repeated or refused boundary violations.

If merged, the house would lose the distinction between place, entry, guest behavior, closure, and consequence.

## Dependencies Found

Boundaries should come before Permission.

Permission should come before Guest Boundary when a guest/helper/file/path asks to enter.

Guest Boundary should feed Return-Home after the guest work is complete.

Enforcement should not fire unless a boundary violation, repeat issue, or refusal appears.

Proof/Receipt should validate moves, facts, and final cleanup.

## Decision Rules Found

Boundaries fires when place/ownership/lane is unclear.

Permission fires when something asks to enter, execute, steer, or be trusted.

Guest Boundary fires when an assistant, mule, helper output, copied file, packet, script, or outside object touches the porch/house.

Return-Home fires at close or after a move/use.

Enforcement fires only after violation or repeat pattern.
