# NEED to KNOW Bases Helper Slice Rule

Status: SUPPORT RULE / ADDENDUM
Authority: not doctrine, not ACTIVE_GUIDES, not CURRENT_TRUTH_INDEX
Parent: HASH_KEY_FRONT_GATE_HELPER_PACKET_ROUTER architecture

## Core line

Only what it needs. Fully what it needs.

## Rule

A helper must not receive the whole house by default. A helper receives only the information needed for its packet, role, authority, proof, and handoff. However, that need-to-know slice must be complete in detail for the helper's actual need.

Need-to-know is not need-to-starve. The helper gets the minimum lane, not minimum detail.

## Required memory shape

One central incident record preserves the whole useful truth once.

Many helper-specific slices are cut from that central record.

Each helper slice must contain every command, input, path, hash, error, false blame, root cause, fix, proof requirement, watch item, and reopen trigger that affects that helper's future behavior.

The Front Gate maps which helpers need which slices and does not dump the whole incident into every helper.

## Required questions

A complete helper slice must answer:

- Why am I receiving this?
- What exact event touches my role?
- What evidence matters to my role?
- What command, input, path, hash, line, output, or receipt do I need?
- What was falsely blamed that I must not repeat?
- What changed in my behavior?
- What must I refuse next time?
- What proof must I produce or respect?
- What stop reason applies if I cannot continue?
- What handoff packet do I create next?
- What condition reopens this?

## Blocked

- no vague helper summaries after a detailed failure exists;
- no whole-house dump into narrow helpers;
- no helper acting from a path without an authority ticket;
- no helper claiming PASS from another helper's proof standard;
- no central incident deletion after slicing;
- no slice without reopen trigger when it came from a failure.

## Close condition

A helper slice passes only when it is narrow enough to avoid bloat and detailed enough to prevent the helper from repeating the old mistake.
