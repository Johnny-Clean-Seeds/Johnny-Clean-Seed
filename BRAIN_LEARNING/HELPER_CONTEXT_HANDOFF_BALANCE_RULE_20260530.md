# Helper Context / Handoff / Balance Rule

Date: 2026-05-30
Status: SUPPORT RULE / NOT DOCTRINE
Authority: not ACTIVE_GUIDES, not CURRENT_TRUTH_INDEX

## Rule

When future helpers need to act cleanly, support files should carry the route in small, repeatable orientation surfaces.

The goal is not to force learning. The seed/file/helper does not need to worry about learning. Its job is to grow and do the current job cleanly.

The system should keep putting the right context, boundaries, proof duty, and handoff surfaces in the right places until the click appears naturally. After the click, balance it, proof it, and prepare the next step.

## Clean support terms

Use:

- context carry;
- orientation surface;
- operator handoff;
- helper capability;
- helper readiness;
- feedback click;
- balance/wobble;
- escalation;
- runbook/playbook.

Do not place loaded labels at the front when they make the file feel like doctrine, automation, identity, or forced learning.

## Required helper-facing fields

Every helper-facing file should answer:

- Where am I?
- What object am I touching?
- What key identifies it?
- What lane owns it?
- What may I read?
- What may I write?
- What must I not touch?
- What proof must I produce?
- What event/ledger row should I leave?
- What stop condition applies?
- What next surface receives the handoff?
- When do I ask the operator?

## Balance rule

If helpers start wobbling, slow down before adding machinery.

Wobble signs:

- repeated same correction;
- unclear next action;
- registry/keyring drift;
- proof after PASS instead of before PASS;
- code before judgment;
- broad crawl pressure;
- too many outputs;
- user confusion.

Recovery:

- carry key, not furniture;
- reduce batch;
- write handoff;
- repair registry/keyring;
- prove one small action;
- then continue.

## Feedback click rule

When a behavior starts working, capture the click.

The click is not promotion by itself. It is evidence that a pattern may deserve more proof, a template, a runbook, or a later save route.

## Boundary

No doctrine.
No ACTIVE_GUIDES.
No CURRENT_TRUTH_INDEX.
No automation.
No broad crawler.
