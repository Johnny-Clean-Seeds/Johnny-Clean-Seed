# Source Roundtable / Big Names and Little Guys Rule

Status: SUPPORT RULE / SOURCE-ORE ROUTING  
Date: 2026-05-23  
Scope: Steiner, Jung, Feynman, Chase Hughes, Jordan Peterson, little-guy sources, practical workers, current ideas

## Rule

Do not let the big-name shelf blind the house.

Big names often provide deep coordinates.

Little guys often provide working handles.

Both can matter.

Use sources by function, not fame.

## Current functional lanes

Steiner:
living form, growth, organism, metamorphosis, phase, observation discipline.

Jung:
meaning-pattern, archetype, inner/outer relation, integration, grandiosity guard.

Feynman:
proof integrity, anti-self-deception, do not perform proof-looking rituals.

Chase Hughes:
behavioral state, baseline, room-reading, needs/fears, influence defense, behavioral decomposition.

Jordan Peterson:
order/chaos, aim, responsibility, narrative map, truthful speech, identity under pressure, meaning through action.

Little-guy sources:
practical handles from people who touch the machine directly: mechanics, nurses, farmers, janitors, builders, operators, teachers, hackers, forum people, YouTube teachers, weird manual writers.

## Clean use

Use each source as source ore.

Do not crown.

Extract:
- method;
- warning;
- handle;
- pattern;
- testable behavior;
- poison/hazard;
- proof lane.

## Hazards

Blocked:
- doctrine import;
- guru authority;
- culture-war noise;
- untested manipulation tactics;
- moralizing instead of proof;
- treating claims as authority without lane and evidence;
- dismissing little sources because they lack status.

## Moving Forward

From now on, source intake should ask:

What function does this source serve?

What does it help the house do better?

What must stay in Fog Zone until proved?

## Short form

Big names give coordinates.

Little guys give handles.

The house needs both.
