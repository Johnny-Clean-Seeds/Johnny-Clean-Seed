## Rule

Repeated surface failures are cause-and-effect evidence, not isolated bugs.

Push can lead to shove. Tit-for-tat means the system is answering back through symptoms. When small failures repeat, mutate, or cluster around the same lower layer, the visible break is a signal from a lower-layer cause.

## Dirty work warning

Do not patch the surface.

A surface patch is dirty, lazy, cheap work when it covers the symptom without repairing the mechanism. It becomes hidden drift: the bandage fades in compression, but the scar remains because it was never fixed.

## Required route

1. Freeze the upper task.
2. Preserve the evidence chain.
3. Scratch-and-sniff from symptom to repeated family.
4. Name the lower-layer cause.
5. Repair the root mechanism, source template, or check gate.
6. Update bounded helpers on a strict need-to-know basis.
7. Resume only after proof.
