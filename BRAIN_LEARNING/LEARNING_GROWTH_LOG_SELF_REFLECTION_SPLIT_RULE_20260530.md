## Rule

When a helper, file, script, gate, map, ledger, or packet has growth logic and encounters an error, it must record the event in two places:

1. The shared `LEARNING_GROWTH_LOG`, where the house sees it as a system learning event.
2. The actor's own self area, where the helper/file/script records it as `I did this` if the actor caused or shaped the error.

Other helpers' errors may be visible as context, but the actor treats those as `life events / circumstances` unless the actor directly caused them.

## Core split

- `SYSTEM_LEARNING_EVENT`: what happened to the chain.
- `SELF_REFLECTION_EVENT`: what this actor did.
- `LIFE_EVENT_CONTEXT`: what happened around the actor but was outside the actor's control.

## Required self-reflection fields

- actor id
- actor type
- event id
- trace id
- packet id
- what I was trying to do
- what I did
- what failed
- exact evidence
- whether this was `I did this` or `life event/circumstance`
- false blames blocked
- correction for my future behavior
- proof required next time
- reopen trigger

## Boundary

Self-reflection improves future behavior. It does not create authority, doctrine, autonomy, or permission to act without a packet.
