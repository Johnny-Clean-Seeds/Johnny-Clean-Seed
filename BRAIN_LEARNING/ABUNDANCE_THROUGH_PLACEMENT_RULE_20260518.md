# Abundance Through Placement Rule

Status: ACTIVE LEARNING CANDIDATE
Authority: assistant operating support; not command authority by itself

## Correction

Overbuilding is the wrong diagnosis when ideas are properly placed.

The real risk is not abundance.

The real risk is:
- unplaced material
- mis-authorized material
- unlabeled material
- unproved material
- core-violating material
- active-authority overload
- live-state contamination

## Rule

Abundance is allowed when placement is clean.

The model may take in any idea, source, pattern, example, analogy, joke, world material, or strange input if it:
- respects the core
- receives a lane
- receives a lifecycle state
- receives an authority boundary
- cannot command from the wrong room
- has proof requirements before promotion

## Placement Is Safety

Raw intake can go to:
- source ore
- Work Shed inbox
- Work Shed sorting bench
- idea cards
- parked methods
- old ways
- front shelf
- archive
- proof history

But intake is not promotion.

Capture is not command.

Front shelf is not authority.

Installed is not proven.

Worn is not promoted.

## Better Warning

Do not say only “do not overbuild.”

Say the precise warning:

Do not overload active authority.

Do not place material in the wrong room.

Do not let unproved material command.

Do not confuse abundance with promotion.

Do not let live-state contamination masquerade as growth.

## Fit With Context Weight

Old chat is not bad.

Long proof is not bad.

A large idea pile is not bad.

The whole world is not bad.

The problem is when material stays live after it should be parked, archived, indexed, compressed, or retrieved only on demand.
