## 2. Major work map

## 2.1 Quantum electrodynamics / QED

Founding work:
Feynman shared the 1965 Nobel Prize in Physics with Sin-Itiro Tomonaga and Julian Schwinger for fundamental work in QED.

Key concepts:
- Space-time view of quantum processes.
- Path integral formulation.
- Feynman diagrams.
- Propagators.
- Renormalization-related reformulation.
- Multiple equivalent mathematical/physical viewpoints.

Clean translation candidates:
- Diagram compression: compress complex interaction into a manipulable visual syntax.
- Many-views rule: different models can describe the same underlying reality; useful views generate different hypotheses.
- Founding path preservation: do not erase blind alleys; they show how the clean result emerged.
- Observable-use rule: a description earns value by how it connects to observation/use.

Candidate rule:
A model is not clean because it sounds elegant. It becomes clean when its symbols, operations, and observation link are defined.

## 2.2 Path integrals / least action

Founding work:
Feynman's Princeton thesis and later QED work used the action principle and path integral picture.

Key concepts:
- Instead of one single route, consider all possible histories/paths.
- Probability amplitudes combine.
- The final behavior emerges through the summed structure.

Clean translation candidates:
- Route cloud before chosen path.
- Many possible flows, one proof-selected route.
- A system can consider many candidate paths without promoting all of them.

Candidate rule:
Open the path space first; promote only the route that survives proof.

## 2.3 Feynman diagrams

Founding work:
Pictorial representation of particle interactions in QED and later quantum field theory.

Key concepts:
- Diagram as computational syntax, not decoration.
- Interaction events become nodes/lines.
- Visual structure helps calculate and think.

Clean translation candidates:
- Map is a working machine when its parts correspond to operations.
- Diagrams should reduce fog, not add art-bloat.
- A diagram is clean only if it tells what can move, interact, transform, or be counted.

Candidate rule:
A picture is useful only when it carries operational syntax.

## 2.4 Superfluid helium

Founding work:
Feynman created a quantum-mechanical account related to superfluid helium behavior.

Clean translation candidates:
- Macro behavior can arise from quantum/micro rules.
- Weird visible behavior often comes from hidden low-level mechanism.
- Do not explain a system at the wrong scale.

Candidate rule:
When the visible behavior is strange, inspect the layer below it.

## 2.5 Weak interactions / V-A theory

Founding work:
Feynman and Murray Gell-Mann contributed fundamental work on weak interactions and beta decay.

Clean translation candidates:
- Correct interaction classification matters.
- Coupling type determines behavior.
- Similar events may differ by hidden interaction structure.

Caution:
Historical priority around V-A theory includes other key figures, especially Sudarshan and Marshak. Do not flatten credit into a hero story.

Candidate rule:
When borrowing a concept, preserve neighbor credit and source complexity.

## 2.6 Parton model

Founding work:
Feynman proposed the parton model for high-energy proton collision processes, helping interpret scattering results and later connecting to quark/gluon structure.

Clean translation candidates:
- Complex object can behave as if made of internal active pieces depending on probe scale.
- The way you probe a system changes what structure becomes visible.
- Internal parts may be operationally real before they are ontologically settled.

Candidate rule:
Probe scale determines which internal parts become useful to model.

## 2.7 Quantum computing / simulation

Founding work:
"Simulating Physics with Computers" argued that simulating quantum physics may require machines that themselves exploit quantum behavior.

Clean translation candidates:
- Wrong substrate makes the model fight the phenomenon.
- A simulator must match the structure of what it simulates.
- Do not force a classical frame onto a non-classical process if the mismatch is the failure.

Candidate rule:
Choose a tool whose structure matches the structure of the problem.

## 2.8 Nanotechnology / room at the bottom

Founding work:
"There’s Plenty of Room at the Bottom" invited direct manipulation and use of matter at small scale.

Clean translation candidates:
- Hidden room exists below the level people usually inspect.
- Big leverage can come from small-scale precision.
- "Bottom" is not low-status; it is where structure is born.

Candidate rule:
When the top is crowded, inspect the bottom layer.

## 2.9 Challenger / reliability and organizational truth

Founding work:
Feynman's Appendix F to the Rogers Commission report pointed to huge mismatches between management and engineering risk estimates.

Clean translation candidates:
- Reality gap detector.
- Management confidence is not engineering proof.
- If risk estimates differ wildly, the system does not know its own safety state.
- A clean system must hear the people closest to failure.

Candidate rule:
When confidence and ground proof disagree, trust the ground proof path.

## 2.10 Teaching / Feynman Lectures

Founding work:
The Feynman Lectures try to rebuild physics from clear conceptual foundations.

Clean translation candidates:
- Teaching is architecture.
- Good explanation starts from mechanism, not vocabulary.
- Conceptual clarity is not simplification by removal; it is structure placed in the right order.
- A first chapter can seed a whole world if ordered correctly.

Candidate rule:
A lesson passes when the learner can use the concept, not merely repeat the words.

## 3. Belief / thought-pattern map

## 3.1 Radical honesty / scientific integrity

Founding source:
Cargo Cult Science.

Core idea:
The appearance of science is not enough. The ritual can look right while missing the essential ingredient: integrity against self-deception.

Clean Seed translation:
No fake PASS. No form-only proof. No performance of rigor.

Candidate rule:
If the planes do not land, the runway ritual is not proof.

## 3.2 Do not fool yourself

Founding source:
Cargo Cult Science.

Core idea:
The scientist must especially guard against self-deception.

Clean Seed translation:
The assistant must run self-checks before declaring closure, promotion, or proof.

Candidate rule:
The first enemy is internal false success.

## 3.3 Doubt experts / science as earned knowing

Founding source:
"What is Science?" and his broader teaching posture.

Core idea:
Science is not respect for authority. It is a method for finding out what is true by experience, observation, testing, and doubt.

Clean Seed translation:
Authority order matters, but authority is not magic. A source must route through proof.

Candidate rule:
Expert words are source ore until checked against mechanism and evidence.

## 3.4 Multiple representations

Founding source:
Nobel lecture and Feynman teaching style.

Core idea:
The same physical reality can be represented by different viewpoints and mathematical descriptions.

Clean Seed translation:
A concept may need several clean maps: diagram, equation, story, failure case, operational command, proof receipt.

Candidate rule:
If a thing is real, it should survive translation into more than one clean representation.

## 3.5 Curiosity with contact

Founding source:
General Feynman life/work style, Los Alamos stories, teaching, practical problem solving.

Core idea:
Curiosity is not passive. It touches the object, tests it, opens it, and plays with it.

Clean Seed translation:
Curiosity becomes clean when it has boundary, proof, and return.

Candidate rule:
Play is clean when it returns with proof.

## 3.6 Anti-polished-result bias

Founding source:
Nobel lecture discussion of blind alleys and sequence of ideas.

Core idea:
Scientific papers often hide the real path. Feynman valued telling the messy sequence of ideas.

Clean Seed translation:
Keep proof history and founding original, not just final cleaned doctrine.

Candidate rule:

## 5. Risk and correction map

Do not import:
- Feynman as a personality cult.
- Feynman as moral authority.
- Feynman anecdotes as verified fact without source.
- "Genius chaos" as an excuse for sloppy behavior.
- Irreverence as permission to ignore boundaries.

- Physics metaphors as project doctrine.

Historical caution:
Some popular Feynman stories come from memoirs and retellings. They may contain useful founding signal, but they need source custody and must not be treated as primary proof unless tied to a reliable source.

Credit caution:
Feynman worked in communities. Preserve co-workers and neighboring contributors:
- Tomonaga and Schwinger for QED Nobel context.
