RESPECT NEIGHBOR RULES / PARTNER CHAIN REVIEW

Cycle placement:
target → authority/key → object/location → action → evidence/proof → verdict/status → next state

Rule:
Do not treat a rule, idea, concept, file, or guide section as isolated when it clearly affects neighbors.

Anchor process:
1. Name the active item.
2. Define its role.
3. Define its key: permission, scope, path, evidence, and allowed action.
4. Identify parent, children, left/right neighbors, and partner rules.
5. Mark each neighbor:
   changed,
   unchanged,
   affected but not changed,
   blocked,
   parked,
   unknown,
   or needs human decision.
6. Check whether the proposed change damages, duplicates, weakens, overrules, or silently expands a neighbor.
7. Put unclear links in Misc / Unplaced.
8. Fix only proven links.
9. Verify anchor and affected neighbors.
10. Close with changed/protected/parked/unknown/next route.

Neighbor-respect principle:
Do not fix one rule by damaging its neighbor.
Do not strengthen one rule by stealing another rule’s authority.
Do not solve one gap by creating overlap.
Do not close one issue while leaving the next linked issue inconsistent.
Do not let a child contradict its parent.
Do not let a parent crush a useful child.
Do not let a sibling silently inherit authority it was not given.
