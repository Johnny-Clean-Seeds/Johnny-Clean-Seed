1. NORMAL FRONT DOOR / GENERAL SYSTEMS DOCTOR
Status: identity concept / product-lane support / parked

The user is a normal-person front door working toward becoming a general systems doctor.

Meaning:
- the user still feels normal-user pain when technical systems expose badly placed words or confusing action layers
- the user is learning enough under-hood logic to diagnose where the problem belongs
- the role is not to become every specialist
- the role is to see the whole body of the system and route symptoms correctly

Clean framing:
The general systems doctor looks across the user layer, programmer layer, AI layer, file layer, proof layer, and product layer, then names the clean next move.

This is useful because the user can translate between ordinary human pain and system structure.

2. WORDS ARE NOT STUPID; WORDS ARE MISUSED OR MISPLACED
Status: word-placement concept / parked

Correction:
Words are never stupid.
Words become dirty when they are misplaced, overloaded, or aimed at the wrong user or layer.

Example:
- push is not stupid inside Git's under-hood world
- push is dirty at the normal-user front door if the user hears it as "put the file where I want it"
- publish online is the cleaner front-door action word for that layer

Clean framing:
The problem is not the word itself.
The problem is placement, overload, audience, and authority level.

3. UNDER-HOOD / FRONT-DOOR TRANSLATION
Status: product/UI concept / parked

Do not latch onto under-hood words as top-layer control language.
Do not stay ignorant of them either.

Better rule:
Know the machine word only enough to map it, then translate it into the clean human-front-door word.

Examples:
- stage = mark for checkpoint
- commit = seal checkpoint
- push = publish online
- branch = split path
- merge = combine work

The danger is not knowing technical words.
The danger is letting technical words become the words normal people have to think in.

4. UNDER-HOOD WORD QUARANTINE
Status: word-routing concept / parked

Misplaced under-hood words can be treated like quarantine material for the front layer.

Meaning:
- technical words may be kept for mapping
- technical words should not automatically become user-facing control words
- the front door should expose human-action language
- under-hood terms can remain visible only when needed for precision

Short form:
Map the machine word.
Do not build the human layer out of misplaced machine words.

5. KNOW / know WORD-KEY CONVENTION
Status: chat-work notation / small-gold word key / not doctrine

The user uses capitalization inside a sentence as power/authority marking.

Convention:
- know = ordinary confidence, "I believe I am right" or "likely true"
- Know = fact-locked, proven enough to act from, "I KNOW this is fact"

This does not need to be formal doctrine unless it later appears inside prompts, guides, or decision gates.

Clean handling:
Respect the notation in chat/work.
Do not turn it into a rule file by default.

6. SMALL GOLD
Status: motto / reminder card / parked

Gold does not have to be huge to be worth keeping.

A small truth, small wording key, small distinction, or small reminder can still be valuable.

Clean framing:
Do not judge value only by size.
Judge by fit, usefulness, placement, and whether it protects the work.

7. IMPERFECT DOES NOT MEAN WORTHLESS
Status: motto / reminder card / parked

A thing can be valuable even when it is incomplete, awkward, rough, or missing an obvious part.

Perfection can become a dirty gate.

Clean reminder:
Imperfect does not mean worthless.
Judge the fit, not only the missing piece.

This is not a license to promote weak material.
It is a warning not to throw away useful truth because it is not fully polished.

9. PUZZLE PILE / UNPLACED VALUE LOT
Status: parking-core refinement / small-gold support concept

When putting a puzzle together, you do not throw away a piece because it does not fit the current spot.

You make special piles:
- edge pieces
- odd colors
- strange shapes
- unique pieces
- duplicate-looking pieces
- pieces that feel related but are not placeable yet

This maps to Clean Seed parking.

Core idea:
A piece can be unplaced without being trash.

Clean framing:
Unique does not mean active.
Unique does not mean gold.
Unique does not mean trash.
Unique means preserve, label, and wait for neighbor proof.

10. FAMILY / NEIGHBOR FIT
Status: parking-core refinement / activation trigger

Some pieces need family before they reveal fit.

A puzzle piece may make no sense alone.
Later, when nearby colors, shapes, edges, themes, or family members appear, the fit becomes obvious.

Clean Seed version:
An idea, file, note, or odd item may need neighboring proof before its role appears.

Activation trigger:
A parked item may move forward when a related family, neighbor, color, shape, proof trail, role, or bridge relationship makes the fit visible.

11. SPECIAL PILES FOR SPECIAL THINGS
Status: parking-system design refinement

The system needs special piles for:
- unique items
- unplaced value
- odd-shaped ideas
- strange wording keys
- partial truths
- orphan source notes
- future-family clues
- not-current but not-trash material

These piles must have labels and capstones so they do not become a junk monster.

Possible lot name:
Unplaced Value Lot

Job:
Hold items that do not fit the current build but may gain meaning when family/neighbors appear.

Allowed:
- rough but promising ideas
- small-gold concepts
- unmatched puzzle pieces
- partial patterns
- weird but memorable notes
- orphan ideas with future-fit potential

Forbidden:
- active authority
- deletion
- promotion without fit proof
- vague hoarding
- unlabeled dumping

12. CLEANUP WARNING FROM PUZZLE MODEL
Status: cleanup safety refinement

Do not delete an unplaced-value item just because it does not fit the current picture.

A deletion decision needs more than "I cannot place this right now."

Better cleanup question:
Is it unplaced value, duplicate/noise, risky/quarantine, retired history, source proof, or proven waste?

If the answer is unknown, park it. Do not trash it.
