# Business Risk View

Status: SUPPORT EVIDENCE / RISK VIEW ONLY

## Business root

EVIDENCE:
- The whole-house verdict says internal truth is richer than the current entry path.
- It names dirty substrate as a business risk because people trust what they can enter.
- The MTER source says polished output can hide missing process and create fake confidence.

INFERENCE:
- Mr.Kleen's business risk is not only whether the model has depth.
- The risk is whether users, buyers, partners, or future workers can inspect what is current, proven, parked, rejected, experimental, or blocked.

## Risk list

### B-001 Entry friction

Signal:
- A new reader cannot quickly tell what is current, what is support, and what is old.

Business harm:
- The project looks chaotic even when the internal logic is real.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Authority Boundary Map plus Event Ledger.

Natural trigger:
- When a support file contains current-looking labels or old next-move wording.

### B-002 Fake confidence

Signal:
- A polished answer appears before raw pile, proof stack, revision pass, and rejection ledger are visible.

Business harm:
- Trust drops when good-looking output cannot show its work.

Sorting countermeasure:
- MTER required for complex synthesis.

Natural trigger:
- Complex plan, list, sorting, ranking, root-cause, business-critical, or process-explanation request.

### B-003 Support surface impersonates command

Signal:
- TODO, status, mule output, receipt, selector, or map sounds current without current authority.

Business harm:
- Work can move in the wrong direction while appearing disciplined.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Authority state field on every substrate record.

Natural trigger:
- Any file uses "next," "current," "recommended," "active," or similar live-looking language.

### B-004 Proof overreach

Signal:
- A receipt or pass verdict is treated as broader than the claim it proves.

Business harm:
- The system overstates reliability.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Claim-scoped proof field.

Natural trigger:
- Any proof item is used to justify adoption, promotion, cleanup, or guide changes.

### B-005 Gold becomes weight

Signal:
- Good rules, mule returns, ideas, receipts, soft suits, and source ore accumulate without placement.

Business harm:
- The system becomes harder to enter as it improves.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Seed Bank, Event Ledger, Rejection Ledger, and Natural Trigger Index.

Natural trigger:
- Useful material is saved but has no placement state or adoption gate.

### B-006 Dirty substrate grows crooked future work

Signal:
- Old history, proof, ideas, and command-looking fragments feed new work without classification.

Business harm:
- Future decisions inherit hidden confusion.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Minimal substrate intake record.

Natural trigger:
- Any future task draws from status/history or proof-history material.

### B-007 Business clarity lags internal truth

Signal:
- The house can explain itself internally but not to a fresh entrant.

Business harm:
- Strong model, weak adoption story.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Entry dignity standard: show current, proven, parked, experimental, rejected, blocked, and why.

Natural trigger:
- When preparing handoff, public explanation, product story, or external review.

### B-008 Method storage mistaken for method firing

Signal:
- A method is named in content but does not control the work.

Business harm:
- Process credibility becomes unverifiable.

Sorting countermeasure:
- Method Harness Index with visible fired/not-fired evidence.

Natural trigger:
- A method is cited as justification for an output.

## Business pass condition

A sorted substrate item is business-safe enough for support use when:

- its authority state is named;
- its proof claim is scoped;
- its parent boss is visible or marked unknown;
- current-looking history is marked historical unless current authority restates it;
- business risk is named when present;
- rejected or blocked moves are visible;
- natural trigger is named if the item should fire later;
- adoption state is not inflated.
