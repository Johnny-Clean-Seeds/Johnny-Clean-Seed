# Common Sense Deep Research - Revision Delta

Date: 2026-05-21
Status: DELTA / SOURCE MATERIAL ONLY

## Raw First Shape

The first obvious answer would have been:

Common sense is practical judgment based on experience and shared by ordinary people.

## Problems In That First Shape

1. It hides the old perceptual meaning.
2. It ignores philosophical first-principle use.
3. It undercounts infant/core knowledge evidence.
4. It forgets theory of mind.
5. It treats culture as either everything or nothing.
6. It misses semantic memory, schemas, scripts, and plans.
7. It fails to explain why common sense is fast.
8. It does not explain why common sense can be wrong.
9. It does not explain why AI has struggled with it.
10. It gives no defeat condition.

## Revised Shape

Common sense is a layered, situated, defeasible fit system: the capacity and background inventory by which a human quickly forms workable judgments about ordinary reality, other agents, language, norms, risk, and action under limited time, limited attention, and incomplete proof.

## What Changed

### 1. From Single Meaning To Layered Term

Before:
Common sense = good practical judgment.

After:
Common sense has historical, philosophical, cognitive, social, pragmatic, and AI meanings that must be separated.

### 2. From Static Knowledge To Operating Stack

Before:
Common sense = facts people know.

After:
Common sense = facts + defaults + schemas + scripts + heuristics + common ground + cultural norms + defeat checks.

### 3. From Universal Claim To Scoped Claim

Before:
Everyone knows it.

After:
Some layers are broadly human; some are community-local; some are domain-expert; some are personally learned.

### 4. From Proof Substitute To Proof-Guided Default

Before:
Use common sense instead of proof.

After:
Use common sense to choose the smallest fit action, then escalate proof when risk or uncertainty demands it.

### 5. From Confidence To Feedback

Before:
It feels obvious.

After:
It is a default until evidence, context, culture mismatch, expert domain, or failed prediction defeats it.

### 6. From AI Benchmark To General Competence

Before:
If a machine passes a commonsense benchmark, it has common sense.

After:
Benchmark success proves task competence only. General common sense needs robust transfer, grounding, context, relevance control, and defeat handling.

## Best Delta Sentence

The revised model changes common sense from "ordinary obviousness" into "ordinary-world fit judgment with source layers and defeat switches."
