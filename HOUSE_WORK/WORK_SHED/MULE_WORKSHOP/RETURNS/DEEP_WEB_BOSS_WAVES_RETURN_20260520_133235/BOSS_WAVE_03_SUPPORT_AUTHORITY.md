## Best Fix

Winner:
Govern/Map/Measure/Manage Support Stamp.

Working shape:
For any support item that tries to steer:
- Govern: who/what has authority here?
- Map: what lane and neighbor files does it touch?
- Measure: what proof exists and what is missing?
- Manage: apply, adapt, park, reject, block, or inspect next.

## Proof To Myself

Why this beats the prior Authority Baseline Stamp:
The baseline stamp labels status. The NIST RMF-style stamp adds movement: govern, map, measure, manage. That is better for Mr.Kleen because support authority problems are not static; the same item can be helpful, risky, or irrelevant depending on the action.

Why it is better:
- Names authority first.
- Forces lane mapping.
- Requires proof measure before disposition.
- Ends with a managed action, not a vague label.

Disproof:
If the stamp becomes a mini-dashboard or appears on every tiny source, it is too heavy. Use only when a support item risks becoming king.

Verdict:
BEST CANDIDATE FOR WAVE 03 / GUARD ONLY.
