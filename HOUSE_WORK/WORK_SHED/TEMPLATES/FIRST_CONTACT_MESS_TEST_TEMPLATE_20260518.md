# First-Contact Mess Test Template

Status: SUPPORT TEMPLATE
Authority: support only; not command authority

## Purpose

Use this template to test whether a guide, tool, workflow, route, explanation, or object gives a first-contact observer enough clarity, care, reward, and safe entry to keep interacting.

This is not an intelligence test.

No one is dumb. The test asks whether the object earned enough care.

Observer mess is design evidence.

Raw observer feeling is source signal, not proof.

## Correct Flow

raw interaction -> observed mess -> pattern capture -> hypothesis -> fix candidate -> proof

## Builder Responsibility Check

Do not blame the observer first.

If the observer disengaged, misused the object, or missed the point, inspect the object first.

Possible design causes:

- the thing did not invite care
- the first step was unclear
- the payoff was hidden
- the language was too internal
- the object asked for too much effort too early
- the observer had no reason to keep trying
- the handoff contaminated or confused them
- the design expected care before it earned care

## Guardrails

- Raw feeling is not proof.
- Observer mess is evidence, not insult.
- No one is dumb; inspect care, clarity, burden, reward, and entry.
- Do not over-brief the observer.
- Do not promote a fix until proof after fix.
- Do not rewrite active guides from this template alone.
