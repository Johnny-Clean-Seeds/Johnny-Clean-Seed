## Why this fits

The recent Grandmaster Node Root Ledger save exposed a lower-level save-order failure while the active object was a higher-level map/design/test object. The first direct save failed because the manifest was treated as a candidate before it existed. That was not a Grandmaster concept problem. It was a lower-level manifest/receipt order problem.

A similar save-order family had just appeared and been repaired in the Scale Chain proof-review route. This means the correct reaction is not just another upper-object V1.1 repair. The lower-layer family itself needs a named freeze rule.

## What this rule adds

- It makes lower-layer errors a Powerplay trigger.
- It blocks clean-looking upper-object repairs that hide base defects.
- It forces evidence capture before rerouting.
- It requires repeated-history checks when a lower-layer family appears more than once.
- It makes upper-task resume conditional on lower-layer proof.
