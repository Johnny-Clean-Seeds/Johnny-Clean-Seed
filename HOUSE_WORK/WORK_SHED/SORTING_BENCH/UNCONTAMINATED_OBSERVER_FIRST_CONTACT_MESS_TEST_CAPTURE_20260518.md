## Source

The user described a suit insight:

People like the user may work best when left out of the formal internal loop at first.

They need to feel the problem directly rather than think they already know what it is.

The user identified over-briefing as dirty milk because it contaminates raw observation.

## Captured Idea Cluster

1. Some observers must stay outside the diagnosis loop at first.
2. Raw feel can expose seams formal analysis misses.
3. Feeling is source signal, not proof.
4. Diagnosis and proof come later.
5. Experts often know too much to see first-use reality.
6. Companies should test with people who know just enough to use the thing and make a revealing, non-destructive mess.
7. The mess is evidence about the design, not a reason to blame the user first.
8. This is like making toys for kids: watch real children interact before mass production.
9. The user already does this with their kids by giving a vague-enough handoff and observing interaction.
10. The method should become a repeatable Clean Seed testing workflow.
