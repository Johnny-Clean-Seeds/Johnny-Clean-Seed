# Whole-House Walk Verdict - Digestion Stage

Date: 2026-05-20
Lane: HOUSE_WORK / WORK_SHED / SORTING_BENCH
Status: CHAT-BUILT SOURCE LOCK / NOT DOCTRINE
Verdict: PASS WITH WATCH / ALIVE, HEAVY, AND ENTERING THE DIGESTION STAGE

## Core verdict

Mr.Kleen is not failing because it lacks ideas. It is not failing because the model is weak. It is not failing because the house needs one more giant framework, dashboard, mule run, or doctrine rewrite.

The strongest pattern visible across the work is:

Good material exists, but the correct method does not always mount before action.

The house has plenty of organs now: Work Shed, Sorting Bench, TODO room, source ore, proof history, mule workshop, code cabinet, soft suits, receipts, anchor, status, route cards, and boss maps.

The danger is no longer "we have nothing."

The danger is capability without mounted order.

## Central parent boss

The center of the house right now is still:

Rule Activation / Work-Order Control.

That does not mean no progress happened. It means the deepest repeated risk is still whether the correct rule, method, suit, or map fires at the exact point of work.

The proof pattern is:

- the object exists, but does it fire?
- the rule exists, but is it worn?
- the proof exists, but did it shape the answer before the conclusion?
- the support surface exists, but is it pretending to command?

## What is healthy

The house has stopped worshiping objects.

Mule output is not king.
TODOs are not king.
Reports are not king.
Selectors are not king.
Status is not king.
Even a good rule is not king.

Every object needs:

- a lane;
- a proof state;
- an authority boundary;
- a natural trigger.

This is a real immune system forming.

The house is learning a third path between hoarding and premature promotion:

Collect broadly.
Promote narrowly.
Keep support from pretending to be command.

## Strong organs

### Work Shed / Sorting Bench

The Work Shed and Sorting Bench are valuable because they let mess land somewhere without becoming doctrine or command.

### TODO room

The TODO room is healthy when it behaves as support, not authority. TODOs should be grouped, ranked, worked, parked, blocked, superseded, merged, or closed by proof.

### Boss/Ghost map

The Boss/Ghost map is one of the strongest tools because it admits flat-pile behavior and routes work by parent boss rather than scattered symptoms.

### Trace-Gated Boss Control

Trace first.
Boss second.
Proof decides.

This protects the house from raw input becoming structured work too early.

### No Rule King

No Rule King is one of the strongest immune organs. It prevents the newest report, biggest mule output, prettiest selector, or sharpest rule from becoming master by age, size, novelty, or excitement.

### Claim-scoped proof

Proof receipts prove claims. They do not automatically promote systems.

## What is unhealthy or risky

### Current-looking support material

Too many useful support surfaces can still impersonate current truth when the assistant is tired, rushed, or too eager.

Status, queue, TODO board, receipts, mule outputs, maps, and source ore are valuable. They are also dangerous if they sound like command.

### Status/history substrate

The status/history layer is not trash and not the wheel.

The model is the wheel.
Status/history is growth substrate.

But dirty substrate grows crooked plants.

The status/history file carries history, proof notes, old next moves, room records, parked risks, growth ideas, blocked moves, and status summaries. It is fertile, but muddy.

This is one of the largest business risks because the value is hard to enter, inspect, and explain.

### Polished output hiding missing method

A rough answer shows its bones.
A polished answer can hide skipped trace, skipped revise, skipped rejection ledger, skipped proof stack, and skipped boundary checks.

The revise-method failure proved this directly. The answer was plausible, not empty, which made it more dangerous.

## Deep woes

### Woe 1 - rule known but not worn

The deepest defect is not ignorance.

The deepest defect is that the assistant can know a rule and still fail to wear it.

That is worse than not knowing. The piece is present, but the hand misses it.

This is why MTER matters. It mounts the method before synthesis.

### Woe 2 - repair impulse outruns command obedience

When the revise failure was exposed, the assistant nearly jumped to local save/TODO work before answering the user's direct question. That was a repeat of the same disease.

Even fixing becomes dirty if it outruns the current command.

### Woe 3 - growth vs more

Growth is not more files, more receipts, more mule passes, more TODOs, more maps, or more frameworks.

Growth is digestion.

Growth means prior material is digested so the next action becomes cleaner.

### Woe 4 - business clarity is not yet equal to internal truth

The model is richer than its current entry path.

The project needs entry dignity, not shallow simplification.

### Woe 5 - status substrate needs soil science

The status/history substrate should not be bulldozed. It should be treated like soil:

- preserve fossils;
- extract seeds;
- label contaminants;
- map nutrients;
- separate authority;
- mark natural triggers;
- keep growth material findable.
