FIRST ROUTE-PICKING UGLY DUCK CANDIDATES
These are not final winners. They are candidate ducks.

1. Reversibility Test / One-Way Door vs Two-Way Door
Plain meaning:
Ask whether a move can be undone cleanly.

Route value:
Sets the proof burden before action.

Clean routing rule:
- Two-way door: smallest useful test is allowed.
- One-way door: stronger proof, backup, and approval required.
- Unknown door: treat as one-way until reversibility is proven.

Why it may be pretty:
It acts as a speed control. It tells the system when to move fast and when to slow down.

Risk if missing:
The system treats all next moves as equal and may rush irreversible work.

Risk if overused:
The system becomes too cautious and avoids useful growth.

2. Bottleneck-First Routing
Plain meaning:
Find the constraint that blocks the most future movement.

Route value:
Chooses the next move by asking what unlocks the most clean progress.

Why it may be pretty:
It prevents polishing side items while the real blocker remains.

Risk if missing:
The system does busy work instead of opening the blocked lane.

Risk if overused:
The system may focus only on constraints and miss creative growth opportunities.

3. Cost-of-Delay / Regret Routing
Plain meaning:
Ask what gets worse if we wait, and what regret grows if we choose wrong.

Route value:
Helps choose between multiple viable next moves.

Why it may be pretty:
It adds time pressure without letting urgency become authority.

Risk if missing:
Important windows may be missed.

Risk if overused:
Urgency may distort judgment and make weak routes look necessary.
