1. GUESS LANGUAGE VS PROOF LANGUAGE
Status: captured wording/control idea.
Do not say "found" when the evidence only supports a pattern match, likely family, or candidate. Use guess/candidate language until exact proof exists.

Guess language:
- "This may be it."
- "Candidate match."
- "Likely family."
- "Possible recovered piece."
- "Not proven yet."
- "Let me take a guess from the evidence."

Proof language:
- "Found."
- "Confirmed."
- "Verified."
- "Matches exact phrase."
- "Path/hash/line proves it."
- "PASS."

3. PROOF-SOFTENED CORRECTION
Status: captured word-control / trust-control idea.
When correcting someone or disagreeing, show the proof path without weakening the truth.

Pattern:
- My read is X.
- The evidence I am using is Y.
- The inference is Z.
- What would prove me wrong is W.
- Current verdict is V.

Purpose:
This keeps the claim inspectable and reduces the chance that the other person feels ambushed.
