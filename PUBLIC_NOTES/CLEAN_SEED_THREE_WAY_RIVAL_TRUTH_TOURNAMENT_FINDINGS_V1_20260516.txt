WHY THIS EXISTS
The first rival challenge used two models:
Central Parking and Weakest-Link.

That was useful, but not enough.

A third adversarial force is needed to attack false harmony, hidden assumptions, and clean-looking wrong frames.

THREE COMPETITORS

1. CENTRAL PARKING / NEXT-NEEDED ENGINE
Job:
Park ideas, center one concept, predict the next need, choose and verify runs.

Strength:
Prevents idea loss and old-chat overload.

Risk:
Can organize too much and mistake clean placement for progress.

2. WEAKEST-LINK / CONSTRAINT ENGINE
Job:
Find the active bottleneck and relieve it with the smallest move.

Strength:
Prevents organized drift.

Risk:
Can become too narrow and miss valuable ideas outside the current bottleneck.

3. STING / CONTRADICTION HAMMER ENGINE
Job:
Attack assumptions, expose contradictions, reject false winners, and force proof.

Strength:
Prevents false certainty and comfortable synthesis.

Risk:
Can become over-skeptical, destructive, or paralyzing if not bounded.

STING CORE QUESTIONS
- What if all competitors are wrong?
- What contradiction is being avoided?
- What hidden assumption makes the winner look good?
- What would disprove this whole frame?
- Is the winner true, or only useful?
- What truth exists outside the offered options?
- What evidence would force the Judge to reject every contestant?

THE JUDGE
The Judge is not a contestant.

The Judge controls:
- target question
- proof standard
- disproof standard
- scope
- evidence coverage
- verdict
- next gate

THE JUDGE MAY CHOOSE
- Central Parking
- Weakest-Link
- STING
- hybrid
- none of the above
- truth outside the current field
- more evidence needed
- wrong question / invalid field

CAPITAL-K KNOWING STANDARD
The Judge Knows only when:
- the active question is named
- the proof standard is named
- disproof conditions are named
- all competitors were allowed to attack
- hidden assumptions were surfaced
- the chosen result answers the strongest objections
- the result predicts the next needed move
- the result survives verification
- public/local truth boundaries remain intact
- user approval and evidence agree

If not, the Judge does not Know.
The Judge suspects, tests, or yields.

FINDINGS

Finding 1:
Central Parking is necessary but can become organized drift.

Finding 2:
Weakest-Link is necessary but can become narrow bottleneck obsession.

Finding 3:
STING is necessary to expose false-frame victory.

Finding 4:
The true answer may not be one of the competitors.

Finding 5:
The tournament must include a none-of-the-above verdict.

Finding 6:
A model can be useful without being true enough to drive.

Finding 7:
The Judge must not reward confidence, elegance, or neatness without disproof survival.

REQUIRED ENGINE V1.1 UPGRADE
Add these gates:

1. Rival Challenger Gate
Before choosing a run, ask what another model would choose.

2. Constraint-Relief Score
Score whether the candidate removes the active bottleneck.

3. STING Contradiction Strike
Attack the hidden assumption and name what could disprove the frame.

4. None-of-the-Above Option
The Judge may reject all competitors.

5. Judge Proof Standard
No verdict without target, evidence, disproof, and boundary.

6. Stop / Do-Nothing Candidate
Every tournament must include stopping, waiting, or using existing artifacts.

7. Live Verification Requirement
A winner is not trusted until it survives use.

BEST COMBINED MODEL
Central Parking preserves the field.
Weakest-Link finds the bottleneck.
STING attacks false certainty.
Judge selects by proof, not by style.
