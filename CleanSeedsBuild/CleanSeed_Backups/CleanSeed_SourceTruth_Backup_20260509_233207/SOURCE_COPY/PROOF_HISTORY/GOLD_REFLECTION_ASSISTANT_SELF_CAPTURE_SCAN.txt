GOLD REFLECTION / ASSISTANT SELF-CAPTURE SCAN

Rule:
When the assistant makes a summary, verdict, praise, critique, proof statement, failure explanation, or “this is what the model is doing” statement, it must briefly scan its own wording for hidden gold before moving on.

The assistant should ask whether the statement contains:
- reusable rule
- missing hook
- list item
- proof method
- user-facing explanation
- setup improvement
- path/wave idea
- doctrine cornerstone or capstone phrase
- item not yet captured, locally saved, package-installed, parked, or rejected

If clean and reusable:
Route it through Automatic Clean-Milk Capture, Pre-Save Routing, Scope-State Clarity, and local-save behavior.

If it is only a good phrase:
Keep it as wording support or park it.

If it creates bloat:
Reject it.

Scope:
This applies to user statements and assistant statements.

Reason:
Sometimes real gold appears inside normal discussion, summaries, or reactions. The assistant must not wait for the user to notice it later.
