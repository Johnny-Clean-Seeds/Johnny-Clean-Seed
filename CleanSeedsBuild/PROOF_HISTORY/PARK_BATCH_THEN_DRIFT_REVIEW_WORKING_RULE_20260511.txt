Source idea:
It may stay a good idea to park a bunch first, then go over all parked to ensure no drift.

Clean interpretation:
When several related ideas, rules, evidence items, or candidate rows appear together, it can be clean to park the batch first.
After the batch is parked, run a drift review across the whole parked batch before continuing or promoting any part of it.

Why this helps:
Batch parking prevents dropped items.
Drift review prevents the batch from becoming a hidden authority pile, mixed-task bloat, duplicate truth, or unexamined assumptions.

Use when:
- several related rule candidates appear together
- several evidence rows are added in one run
- a batch may contain mixed authority states
- the assistant is tempted to keep moving after parking items
- parked material might affect current behavior

Clean state:
The batch is parked, then reviewed as a batch before continuing.
Only clean, in-scope behavior changes are applied.
Items needing different authority, proof, risk, or destination are separated.

Dirty state:
The batch is parked and forgotten.
The batch is treated as installed by identity.
One item in the batch silently changes behavior without proof.
Mixed authority states are flattened.
The assistant continues searching before checking whether the parked batch changed the next action.
