ONE-HAND GOLD HANDLING RULE

Status:
Local durable save.
Not installed into ACTIVE_GUIDES yet.
Created to stop gold loss during Recycle Bin, lost-folder, proof-history, and rule-recovery work.

Core rule:
Gold cannot be carried in working memory.
Gold must be placed visibly before continuing.

One-hand limit:
One unbanked gold item is the maximum.
If one gold item appears, stop long enough to record it.
If a second gold item appears before the first is recorded, YIELD or pause the active task until the first is banked/statused.

Boulder-first rule:
Boulders outrank gold bars.
Gold bars outrank nuggets.
Nuggets outrank loose interesting ideas.
Dirty milk risk is captured separately and must not be mixed into clean gold.

No invisible gold rule:
Do not say “got it,” “saved,” “locked,” or “we have it” unless the gold is visible in the tray, locally saved, or explicitly marked chat-only with reason.

Gold loss trigger:
If gold is noticed but not recorded, treat that as a leak.
Stop and repair the capture path before continuing.

Guard trigger:
If one-hand handling still leaks gold, add a guard layer before continuing recovery work.
A guard layer means a separate visible checkpoint that checks:
- all gold IDs are present
- all gold has a status
- no unbanked gold remains
- return point is written
- next action is single-target

Recycle Bin use:
During Recycle Bin recovery, every recovered item starts as ore.
Useful findings become nuggets only after content review.
Repeated or strong nuggets become bars only after evidence review.
Stamped bars require ID, source, placement, status, and leak-check route.
