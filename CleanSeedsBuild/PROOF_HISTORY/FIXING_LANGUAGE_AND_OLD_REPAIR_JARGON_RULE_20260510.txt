FIXING LANGUAGE + OLD REPAIR-JARGON BUBBLE RULE

Status:
Local durable save.
Not installed into ACTIVE_GUIDES yet.
Created during Gold Pan / trash-digging recovery work.
Purpose: stop rough repair jargon from making Clean Seed workflow sound sloppy, unsafe, or automatic.

Core gold rule:
Pile-making is not judging.
Judging is not fixing.
Fixing is not deleting.

Meaning:
Pile-making means sorting a messy evidence pile into visible groups by family, role, source, path, type, risk, or status.
Judging means deciding value, trust, status, risk, route, or next action.
Fixing means changing or improving the correct active file, rule, guide, wording, or workflow.
Deleting means a separate proof-and-approval gate.

Control rule:
Do not let pile-making become judgment.
Do not let judgment become fixing.
Do not let fixing become deletion.

Trash Dig / Pile-First Recovery:
When working through Recycle Bin material, lost folders, duplicate guides, proof-history clutter, recovered quarantine files, or messy evidence piles, do not inspect/fix/delete item-by-item in the raw mess.
First freeze the scene.
Then inventory.
Then make piles of like things.
Then review one pile at a time.
Then route each pile to active truth, proof history, archive, parked reference, blocked evidence, duplicate/no-new-value, or delete gate.

Preferred wording:
Use fixing, repair, correction, guide update, active-file change, install, or refinement depending on the exact action.

Old repair-jargon bubble rule:
Do not casually use the word “patch” in normal Clean Seed workflow language.
If the word “patch” appears because it is inside an old filename, old ID, recovered marker, tool output, or exact quote, place it inside a clear warning/definition bubble and define it as old historical repair/update jargon.

Required bubble:

==================== OLD REPAIR-JARGON MARKER ====================
"PATCH" here means: an old/historical repair or update label found in a file name,
ID, recovered note, tool output, or exact quote.

It does NOT mean:
- quick hackjob
- sloppy fix
- automatic install
- active guide truth
- permission to change files
- permission to delete anything
